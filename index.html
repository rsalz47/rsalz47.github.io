Hey there. I'm a reverse engineer by trade and a tech junkie by choice.

You might've seen me around the net as Swillion. I've got two fancy papers in Computer Science from The University of Massachusetts, Amherst that say I'm certified to sit behind a screen, and I've developed a great deal of expertise in that regard. My professional interests lie in low-level security along the software-hardware line, with preference tipping slightly towards the former. I have projects that focus on system design and implementation, such as a software simulation of a MIPS-1 CPU, a tree-walk interpreter written in Java, and a rudimentary kernel. I also think giant robots are pretty damn sick.

On the left hand side, you can see a sidebar that helps you navigate to the other slices of my site. The Projects tab contains writeups and repository links for any technical work I think is worth sharing; the Blog tab is for anything I'd like to talk about, whether that be my reaction to a movie I've seen or short story ideas that I want to write down; the Gunpla tab has pictures of my latest hobby, building and customizing plastic models of the aforementioned awesome giant robots. I'll post site updates on this main page, so check in once in a while to see what's new. A little company's always great.
